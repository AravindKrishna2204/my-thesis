Introduction

The problem this thesis solves

CAR-T cell therapy has revolutionised treatment of haematological malignancies, but a critical manufacturing bottleneck remains — generating sufficient numbers of functional, non-exhausted T cells outside the human body. Traditional protocols rely on soluble IL-2, which drives terminal differentiation and exhaustion, and requires expensive repeated supplementation. This thesis proposes anchoring IL-7 directly onto K562 feeder cell surfaces to create a reusable, physiologically relevant expansion platform.

The challenge
Soluble cytokine protocols (IL-2) cause T cell exhaustion and terminal differentiation, reducing therapeutic efficacy. Manufacturing costs remain a major barrier to clinical accessibility.
The solution
Membrane-anchored IL-7 on K562 feeder cells provides sustained, localised cytokine signalling without repeated soluble supplementation — mimicking natural immune microenvironment cues.
Research site
Synthetic Immunology Laboratory, Division of Cancer Research, Rajiv Gandhi Centre for Biotechnology (RGCB), Thiruvananthapuram — under Dr. Sunil Martin, Scientist E-II.
Significance
This platform is modular, scalable, and cost-effective — with direct applicability to CAR-T manufacturing pipelines, G-Rex bioreactor systems, and NK / γδ T cell expansion.
Aims & Objectives

What this study set out to achieve

The central aim was to generate a K562 feeder cell line expressing membrane-bound IL-7 (mbIL-7), growth-arrest it with Mitomycin C, and establish an efficient T cell expansion platform.

01
Cloning of membrane-bound IL-7 construct
Replace IL-15 insert in the EHT backbone vector with the IL-7 coding sequence using XbaI / NheI restriction sites, retaining CD28 hinge and transmembrane domains.
Completed
02
Generation of stable MbIL-7 K562 cell line
Package the construct into 3rd-generation pseudolentiviral particles via HEK293T, transduce K562 cells, and enrich by FACS sorting on GFP co-expression.
Completed
03
Optimisation of K562 growth inhibition by Mitomycin C
Test 1, 5, and 10 µg/mL concentrations over 8 days to identify the dose that achieves proliferation arrest while preserving feeder cell viability for co-culture.
Completed
04
Functional testing of engineered K562 for T cell expansion
Co-culture optimised MbIL-7-K562 feeders with primary T cells; assess fold expansion, phenotype, cytokine production (IL-2, IFN-γ), and cytotoxicity. CAR-T cell support is the future application.

Materials & Methods

Experimental workflow

The study followed a sequential workflow from gene cloning to cell line generation and functional characterisation.

RNA isolation (PBMC) cDNA synthesis Gradient PCR optimisation IL-7 large-scale amplification XbaI / NheI restriction digestion IL-7 MB plasmid construction DH5α transformation & colony PCR Midi-prep plasmid isolation HindIII digestion validation 3rd-gen lentiviral packaging (HEK293T) Viral titration by GFP expression K562 transduction (MOI optimised) FACS sorting — BD FACSAria III Cryopreservation of stable line RT-qPCR (ΔΔCt, β-actin reference) Mitomycin C proliferation assay Trypan blue viability tracking × 8 days
Key construct design: MbIL-7 was built by replacing the IL-15 insert in an existing MbIL-15 plasmid with the IL-7 coding sequence, while retaining the CD28 hinge region and CD28 transmembrane domain — ensuring stable surface expression and localised IL-7 delivery without secretion into the bulk culture medium.
Results

Key findings

All three completed objectives were achieved with high confidence. Below are the main results from each experimental phase.

🧬
IL-7 amplification & plasmid validation
Gradient PCR optimised annealing at 50°C — yielding a single, distinct IL-7 band confirming primer specificity. The recombinant plasmid was validated by three independent methods: (1) XbaI/NheI insert release at expected size, (2) HindIII digestion matching in silico predictions from SnapGene, and (3) IL-7 primer PCR amplicon of the anticipated size. All three approaches collectively confirmed successful cloning.
🔬
FACS enrichment of transduced K562 cells
Lentiviral transduction achieved approximately 24% initial GFP expression. After FACS sorting (BD FACSAria III, sterile conditions), purity increased to 98.23% GFP-positive. Cells were gated by forward/side scatter to exclude debris and dead cells. The sorted population was expanded and cryopreserved for downstream functional assays.

RT-qPCR confirms stable MbIL-7 mRNA expression
Using the ΔΔCt method normalised to β-actin (housekeeping gene), MbIL-7 mRNA in transduced K562 cells showed a mean fold change of 4.104 ± 0.778 relative to untransduced controls. This result was statistically significant and stable across multiple cell passages — validating stable transgene integration and confirming the engineered K562 line as a reliable, durable feeder system.

🛡️
Mitomycin C proliferation arrest optimisation
Three concentrations tested over 8 days against untreated controls. Both 5 µg/mL and 10 µg/mL achieved highly significant proliferation suppression (p < 0.0001). Cell viability was largely maintained at 5 µg/mL through Day 6; at 10 µg/mL a significant viability reduction occurred by Day 8 (p < 0.001). The 5–10 µg/mL range was identified as optimal.
